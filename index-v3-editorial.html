Raspberry Pi (Home Server)

Self-Hosted — DIY Hardware

Hosting OpenClaw on a Raspberry Pi 5 at home is a popular project in the community. After the initial hardware cost of approximately $60 to $80, the only ongoing expense is electricity — roughly $3 per month.

It is a great learning project, but not recommended as your primary OpenClaw host if you rely on it daily.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the minimum server requirements to run OpenClaw?
OpenClaw requires at least 1 vCPU, 2 GB RAM, 20 GB storage, and Node.js 22.14 or later (Node 24 recommended). Any modern Linux distribution works. For comfortable daily use with multiple channels and skills, we recommend 2 vCPU and 4 GB RAM as the ideal baseline.
Is OpenClaw hosting free?
OpenClaw itself is free and open-source software released under the MIT license. The hosting cost depends on your chosen platform — Ampere.sh offers a genuinely free tier with $0 per month, Hetzner VPS starts at approximately €4.50 per month, and enterprise options like AWS start around $8 to $15 per month. You will also need your own AI model API key from Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, or another supported provider.
What is the best OpenClaw hosting for beginners?
Ampere.sh is the clear recommendation for beginners. It is the only fully managed OpenClaw hosting platform — your agent is pre-installed and ready to use in under 60 seconds. No Linux command line, no server configuration, no reverse proxy setup. The free tier makes it risk-free to try.
Can I move my OpenClaw from one host to another?
Yes. OpenClaw is fully portable. All configuration, memory, personality, skills, and session data live in a single folder (~/.openclaw/). Copy that folder to any new server, run OpenClaw, and your agent picks up exactly where it left off — including its memory, personality, and tool connections. The entire migration takes about 10 minutes.
Is my data private on managed OpenClaw hosting?
On Ampere.sh, each user receives a fully isolated private environment. The platform cannot read your conversations, access your files, or view your agent's memory. No user data is shared with third parties, and conversations are never used for AI model training. The platform has undergone professional security auditing.
Do I need my own AI API key for OpenClaw?
Yes, regardless of which hosting option you choose. OpenClaw connects to AI model providers — including Anthropic (Claude), OpenAI (GPT-4), and Google (Gemini) — using your personal API key. This means you maintain full control over model selection, usage costs, and rate limits independently from your hosting costs.
How is OpenClaw different from ChatGPT or Claude?
ChatGPT Plus and Claude Pro are conversational chat interfaces. OpenClaw is a self-hosted autonomous agent platform. The key differences: OpenClaw connects to your actual tools and services, takes real actions (booking appointments, sending emails, filing bugs), runs continuously 24/7, maintains persistent long-term memory, and works across WhatsApp, Discord, Telegram, Slack, and 50+ other channels simultaneously. It is closer to a full-time AI employee than a chat window.
Which is cheaper for OpenClaw — Hetzner or DigitalOcean?
Hetzner is significantly more affordable. Their CX22 plan (2 vCPU, 4 GB RAM, 40 GB NVMe) costs approximately €4.50 per month. A comparable DigitalOcean Droplet (2 GB RAM) costs $12 per month — nearly three times the price for similar specifications. For budget-conscious self-hosters, Hetzner delivers the best value by a wide margin.
